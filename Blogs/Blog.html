It is based on the idea that systems can learn from data, identify patterns, and make decisions with minimal human intervention. There are three main types of machine learning: supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning.

Siamese Network / Oneshot Learning

A Siamese network is a type of neural network that consists of two or more identical sub networks, which share weights and biases. The sub networks are connected by a layer that combines their output, and the whole network is trained to perform a specific task. One common application of Siamese networks is in the field of computer vision, where they are used for tasks such as image matching and verification. In these tasks, the Siamese network is given two or more images as input, and it compares them to determine whether they are the same or different. For example, the network might be trained to identify whether two images are of the same person or not.

Open CV

OpenCV (Open Source Computer Vision) is a free and open-source library of computer vision and machine learning algorithms designed to help developers build and deploy computer vision applications. It contains over 2500 optimized algorithms for image and video analysis, including object detection, recognition, and tracking. OpenCV was developed by Intel and is now maintained by a team of developers led by Gary Bradsky. It is written in C++, with interfaces available for Python, Java, and other programming languages
